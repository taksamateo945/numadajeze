What is Hello Wordl?

“Hello Wordl” is a copy of Josh Wardle’s hugely successful Wordle program. It is created by Belgian programmer and musician @chordbug, who is active on Twitter. He calls it a word-guessing game that is “less social but indefinitely replayable.”

The Hello Wordl website has the following description about the game:

The primary difference between this game and Wordle is that you can choose the number of letters the mystery word will have. The default setting for the bar, which was taken from the first game, is “5”. However, by by adjusting the slider, you can select from 4 to 11 letters.

The regulations are basic other from that. Similar to Wordle, you must correctly identify a mystery word in six tries or less. The fact that you can play the game as often as you like while Wordle only allows for one placement every day is another crucial component of it.

You’re under no obligation to tweet about your successful guess of the secret word. Play the game solely for your own enjoyment. However, you may still take a screenshot and upload it to your profile.

There are two modes available on Hello Wordl: Hello Wordl Today’s and Hello Wordl Random. The character restriction can still be set in the first mode, which is similar to Wordle in that everyone is guessing the same word.

The second mode, which is more akin to Wordle Unlimited, enables unlimited game play. Simply guess a surprise word repeatedly, go through the motions again, and keep doing so. There are no restrictions; the only requirement is that you predict each word in six tries or less.

How to Play Hello Wordl?


You may play the web-based game Hello Wordl right in your browser. Launch any online browser on your computer or smartphone, then go to this page to play. You will see the Hello Wordl Random mode on your screen after it has loaded.

Click “Today’s” in the top left corner of the screen to access the Hello Wordl Today’s mode. You can also specify a maximum number of characters for the mystery word in the game. The slider can be adjusted from four to eleven characters.

You can either use your keyboard or the on-screen keyboard to begin entering words after choosing the mode you wish to play in and the character restriction.

You must first enter a random word. Make sure to pick it carefully and avoid using a term with uncommon letters like Q, X, or Z. The color of the tiles will then adjust based on your input.

The correct letter is in the proper location if the tile turns green; otherwise, it is in the incorrect location if it changes yellow; and finally, it is absent from the word if it turns grey. You can guess the mystery word if you just keep making guesses based on the tile colors.

How is it different than Wordle?

The similarities between Wordle and Wordl are minimal. In fact, the inventor acknowledges that it heavily borrows from it and created it as a Wordle alternative for those who wish to play with it nonstop. The game stands apart, nevertheless, due to a few unique peculiarities.

The main distinction between the two games is that Hello Wordl’s Random mode enables players to play indefinitely, whereas Wordle is only accessible once beginning at midnight.

Another significant distinction is that Hello Wordl lets users specify the maximum number of characters the surprise word can have. Any word between four and eleven characters can be conjectured by players. Wordle only provides a five-letter mystery word to guess, though.

Other than that, the concepts of both games are essentially the same. In the already lengthy collection of games like Heardle, Quordle, Worldle, etc., Hello Wordl is yet another fantastic clone.
